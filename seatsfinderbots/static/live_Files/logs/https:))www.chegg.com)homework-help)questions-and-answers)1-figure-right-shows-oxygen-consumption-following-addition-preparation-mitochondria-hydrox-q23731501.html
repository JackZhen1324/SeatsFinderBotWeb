Best Answer:

When mitochondria are added utilization of β-hydroxybutyrate starts.This utilization has following steps:

A). 1st of all β-hydroxybutyrate convert into acetoacetate by using β-hydroxybutyrate dehydrogenase and next convert into Acetoacetyle CoA by succinyl CoA:3-oxoacid CoA transferase.This Acetoacetyle CoA converts into acetyl CoA by thiolase. Acetyl CoA enters in the normal TCA. NADH and FADH2 enter into the electron transport chain (ETC)to generate ATP. oxygen is utilized in ETC and due to active ETC oxygen starts depleting. as shown in fig a.

B) In ETC, oxygen utilization is coupled with ATP synthesis and ATP synthesis requires ADP and phosphate as substrate, the active ETC get slow down as the amount of ADP depleted due to conversion into ATP (fig b)

C) As the ADP is supplied increase (fig C), the ATP synthesis increase and the coupled reaction of ETC to use oxygen and formation of H2 O increases.

D) With the time the substrate amount remains less due to which TCA get slow down and resulting in stream reaction also slow down. And the slow rate of oxygen consumption observed.
